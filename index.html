TOCA vs LOB Peer Benchmarking

How does BMO's workforce composition compare to the Canadian Big 6?

Prepared by MIA Publicly Available Data Only February 2026 Classification: Public
The Question

Does BMO carry too much support overhead?

BMO's Return on Equity trails Canadian peers by 300–550 basis points. One hypothesis: BMO carries a higher proportion of non-revenue-producing headcount (Technology, Operations, Corporate Areas — or TOCA) relative to Line of Business (LOB) headcount. This analysis tests that hypothesis using only public data.

The Signal

Corporate Segment Expenses Tell the Story

While no bank publishes TOCA headcount, they all report segment-level non-interest expense (NIE). The “Corporate” or “Other” segment captures support function costs — and BMO’s is an outlier.

Why the huge disparity? BMO retains most Technology & Operations costs in Corporate Services, while peers (especially TD and RBC) allocate them to LOBs. The raw percentages are not directly comparable. BMO’s 32.6% does not mean it has 10× more support staff than TD (1.5%) — it means the cost sits in a different bucket. The next section normalizes for this.

Bank of the West Integration BMO’s TOCA ratio is likely temporarily elevated. The acquisition (Feb 2023, +9,300 employees) drove Corporate Services NIE up 15.1% YoY in FY2025. Pre-acquisition TOCA was likely closer to 28–30%. Q4 FY2025 included $202M severance for ~700 reductions, signaling ongoing optimization.

TOCA optimization could contribute 30–40 bps (~$370M pre-tax) toward closing the ROE gap. This is meaningful but addresses only 10–13% of the 310 bps gap to RBC. The remaining gap is driven by revenue mix, credit costs, U.S. integration drag, and capital allocation.

How We Got Here

Methodology & Assumptions

Three independent methods were triangulated. No single method is reliable alone; together they narrow the range significantly.

1

Corporate Segment NIE Proxy

Each bank’s “Corporate/Other” NIE ÷ Total NIE. Normalized for allocation differences: BMO ×0.95, RBC +15%, TD +20%, CIBC +10%, BNS +15%, NA +12%.

Normalized TOCA NIE %
= Raw Corp % ± Allocation Adj.
2

Technology Spending Anchor

Where tech spend is known, estimate tech headcount then gross up for total TOCA.

Tech HC = Spend × 65% ÷ Avg Comp
TOCA = Tech HC × 1.4x
3

Industry Benchmarks

Large universal banks: 28–38% support staff (McKinsey, Accenture, Oliver Wyman). Positioned each bank by complexity, M&A status, tech intensity.
