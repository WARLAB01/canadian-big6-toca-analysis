# Canadian Big 6 — TOCA vs LOB Peer Benchmarking

**Prepared by MIA | Classification: Public | February 2026**

An analysis of workforce composition across Canada's Big 6 D-SIB banks, examining the ratio of Technology, Operations, and Corporate Areas (TOCA) headcount to Line of Business (LOB) headcount.

## Key Finding

BMO's estimated TOCA ratio of **~33%** is the highest among Canadian peers (peer median ~28%), implying a LOB:TOCA ratio of **2.0:1** vs RBC's 2.6:1. Reducing TOCA to peer levels would yield ~$370M in annual pre-tax savings (~30–40 bps of ROE).

## Methodology

Three independent Fermi estimation methods were triangulated:

1. **Corporate Segment NIE Proxy** — Using each bank's reported "Corporate/Other" segment expenses as a % of total NIE, normalized for allocation methodology differences
2. **Technology Spending Anchor** — Estimating tech headcount from disclosed tech spend, then grossing up for total TOCA
3. **Industry Benchmark Triangulation** — Positioning each bank within the 28–38% range from McKinsey, Accenture, and Oliver Wyman banking studies

## Data Sources

All analysis is based on publicly available financial disclosures. No internal BMO data was used. Key sources include Q4 earnings releases, supplementary financial information packages, annual reports, and sustainability reports from each bank's investor relations website, plus industry benchmarks from McKinsey, Accenture, Oliver Wyman, and Bank of Canada.
